# Impeccable Design Context

## Design Context

### Users
Tech and data science recruiters, plus general hiring managers. They visit the portfolio to quickly assess Joel's skills, projects, and personality. They scan on desktop primarily. The job to be done: decide within 30–60 seconds whether Joel is worth reaching out to.

### Brand Personality
**Three words**: Creative, professional, clean.

The portfolio should feel like a person with sharp technical depth who also has aesthetic sensibility — not just another CS grad resume. It should be easy to skim but reward slower reading. The tone is confident but not arrogant, personal but not oversharing.

**Emotional goal**: Visitors should feel "this person is both competent AND has a distinct voice" — the rare combination of creative and rigorous.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Theme**: Black and white base with strategic accent color(s) to create pop — move away from the current dark navy + cyan-green which reads as generic "developer dark mode."
- **Reference**: huyml.co — minimalist, authentic, sophisticated motion design, custom typography, humanized identity. Key qualities to borrow: deliberate restraint, personality in copy and motion, non-generic feel.
- **Anti-reference**: Generic dark gloomy portfolios with no personality — the typical dark background + neon accent + card grid pattern. No AI slop aesthetics.
- **Motion**: Purposeful, smooth animations that feel crafted not templated. Personality through motion, not decoration.
- **Avatar**: A character/illustration that sits at the side of the layout while the user navigates between tabs — a signature visual element that persists and gives the portfolio a human face.

### Design Principles
1. **Restraint with personality** — less is more, but what's there should have a point of view
2. **Typography does the heavy lifting** — strong type hierarchy replaces decorative clutter
3. **Motion reveals character** — animations should feel authored, not auto-generated
4. **Recruit-scannable** — recruiters can read role/skills/projects in under 30 seconds
5. **Memorable over safe** — one thing a recruiter remembers after closing the tab
