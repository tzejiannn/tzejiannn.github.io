# Impeccable Design Context

## Design Context

### Users
Primarily Joel himself — this is a personalised creative space that reflects who he is, not just a recruiter-optimised portfolio. Secondary audience: tech and data science recruiters, hiring managers, and anyone curious about Joel's work. Recruiters scan on desktop in 30–60 seconds; but the site should reward slower, more personal reading too. The job to be done varies: recruiters assess fit, others explore personality and creative voice.

### Brand Personality
**Three words**: Creative, rigorous, personal.

The portfolio is a reflection of Joel's identity — someone with sharp technical depth AND genuine aesthetic sensibility. It should feel like a space he actually inhabits, not a polished façade built for job applications. The tone is confident but human, considered but not cold. It rewards people who look closely.

**Emotional goal**: Visitors should feel "this person has a real point of view" — the rare combination of creative and technically rigorous. Not just competent, but interesting.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Theme**: Black and white base with strategic accent color(s) to create pop — warm off-white surfaces, near-black text, deliberate restraint.
- **Reference**: huyml.co — minimalist, authentic, sophisticated motion design, custom typography, humanized identity. Key qualities to borrow: deliberate restraint, personality in copy and motion, non-generic feel.
- **Anti-reference**: Generic dark gloomy portfolios with no personality — the typical dark background + neon accent + card grid pattern. No AI slop aesthetics. Nothing that looks like it was built for recruiters first and Joel second.
- **Motion**: Purposeful, smooth animations that feel crafted not templated. Personality through motion, not decoration.
- **Avatar**: A character/illustration that persists on the right side of the layout across panels — a signature visual element that gives the portfolio a human, personal face. Reserved as a right-column element (240px) matching the home page grid pattern.
- **Typography**: Libre Baskerville (serif) for expressive section headers and editorial moments; Bricolage Grotesque for body and UI. The serif/sans tension creates an editorial, considered feel.

### Layout Pattern
All panels use a consistent two-column structure:
- **Left**: main content (fills remaining space)
- **Right**: 240px avatar column (reserved, consistent across Home, Projects, Resume)
- **Mobile**: single column, avatar column hidden

### Design Principles
1. **Personal before professional** — this is Joel's space first, a portfolio second
2. **Restraint with personality** — less is more, but what's there should have a strong point of view
3. **Typography does the heavy lifting** — strong type hierarchy and serif/sans contrast replace decorative clutter
4. **Motion reveals character** — animations should feel authored, not auto-generated
5. **Memorable over safe** — design choices should make someone pause, not nod along
